    • What is a Core Beliefs Profile?

      The Core Beliefs Profile is a simple 5-10 minute survey-like questionnaire designed to determine the level of alignment between your views on educational mission and culture and that of the school administration, and speaks to the likelihood that a teacher will be happy at that school long term. myEDmatch will use the results of your Core Beliefs Profile to suggest schools with similar educational missions, beliefs, and goals as your own.

      The questionnaire includes seven core areas of education:

      • Mission - a shared set of beliefs around the purpose, goals, and aspirations of a school
      • Instruction - a specific approach to teaching and learning used in a school
      • Professional Development - support of teachers' professional growth and advancement
      • Planning - organization around the task of teaching and learning
      • Student Culture - students' shared beliefs, values, and behaviors concerning school
      • School Environment - the management of time, people, and resources through systems and structures

      Within each area is a series of statements, about which you will select the degree to which you agree or disagree. To do so, just click on the radio button indicating your level of agreement for each statement. Don't forget to click Save after completing each core section. It should take you no more than 5-10 minutes to complete. Once you've completed this profile, you can begin searching for jobs that are right for you using our more than 20 filters via the "Jobs" tab!



    • How do I build my Professional Profile?

      Whether you're looking for your first job, your next job, or a place to showcase the awesome work you're doing in your current job, building a visually-compelling digital professional portfolio is the place to start. In addition to the basics, like detailing your education and experience, your professional profile lets you showcase yourself to schools and other teachers in a way a traditional resume can't. And the best part is, you can build it quickly and easily by following the steps below.


      ExpertTip: If you complete your profile, as reflected by the profile completion bar in the top-right corner, you can unlock premium features, which include the following: Match Scores, messaging with schools, and the ability to see which schools have favorited you. A complete profile is at least one entry in each of the 10 profile sections. Learn more >>


      Step 1: Complete the 'header' section by adding a profile picture, First Impression, and resume. This section of your profile is important because it's the first thing school recruiters and principals see when they view your profile. In other words, it's your virtual first impression.

      To add your profile picture, hover over the default image and click Upload Profile Picture (#1).

      To write your First Impression, click edit, which will open up a text field where you can craft a 140-character introduction (#2). This is a great place to share what you stand for!

      To upload your resume, click on the Upload Resume button (#3). A blue icon will appear below the button when your resume has been successfully uploaded.


      Step 2: Add your work, education, and leadership experience. Continue building your professional profile and portfolio by adding your personal details, including photos and videos, in each section. Just click Add New or Upload in the upper-right corner of each section. Make sure to hit Save then Done when uploading content.


      ExpertTip: Whether in the classroom, your previous career, or in your undergraduate program, school leaders want to know where you've made measurable impact. Just as important, they want to see evidence of a growth mindset. So, share your accomplishments as well as your goals for professional development.



      Step 3: Add endorsements from administrators, colleagues, professors, even students and parents. When principals and recruiters hear: "Hire, [Insert Your Name Here]!" from people who know your work, they get excited. So, the Endorsements section of your profile makes it easy for you to invite others to vouch for your skills.

      Just click Request Endorsements and then select from whom you'd like to receive an endorsement.



      When you click Request Endorsement again, myEDmatch automatically sends an email to the selected individual(s) inviting him/her to write a quick endorsement.

      Step 4: (Optional) Link to your DonorsChoose.org account. Click Add in the upper-right corner of the portfolio section under Leadership. Enter your DonorsChoose.org ID number.

      If you don't know your number, log into your DonorsChoose.org account, place your cursor over you name in the upper-right corner, and click Account. Scroll to the bottom of the page where your Teacher ID Number is listed. Copy it over to your myEDmatch profile, and you're all set!

      Step 5: (Optional) Ask a mentor to review your profile and make notes for improvement. Click Share Private Link with Mentor and you can either retrieve your personal myEDmatch URL to share directly with your mentor or myEDmatch can send a request via email for you.

    • How does matching work?

      Match scores represent the degree of similarity or disparity between a teacher's and school's educational alignment within seven core areas of education: mission, instruction, professional development, planning, student culture, staff culture, and school environment.

      This alignment is assessed using the Core Beliefs Profile, which both teachers and schools complete upon joining myEDmatch, and speaks to the likelihood that a teacher will be happy at that school long term.

      Match Scores are represented by apples, with five apples suggesting a complete beliefs match and zero apples suggesting a complete beliefs mismatch.       Yes. When you register, be sure to indicate the name of your current school employer and that you'd like to be hidden from that school's search results.

    • How do I add student achievement data to my profile without violating privacy laws?

      You're probably aware of The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. Learn more about FERPA here. It's possible, however, to communicate your student achievement results without disclosing the names of individual students. We recommend either sharing your classes' aggregate results (i.e. 93% of students scored a 4 or higher on the Kansas State Reading Assessment) or uploading data trackers without names or with faux names.

      You can keep track of the jobs you've applied to from Homeroom > Jobs You're Interested In or Jobs > Interested tabs. As schools update the status of your application, it will be reflected in each of these places. You'll also receive an email from myEDmatch.
