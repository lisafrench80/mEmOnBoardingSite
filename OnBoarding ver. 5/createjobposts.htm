Though this is traditionally a cumbersome process, we’ve made creating and posting jobs quick and easy on myEDmatch. If you have more than 10 jobs to post, ask your Customer Success Manager, Tom Clark (Tom.Clark@myedmatch.com) to send you a spreadsheet outline of job information we need so we can help you get your initial set of jobs into the system. You can also easily create jobs in the system directly by doing the following:


Go to step 1

Step 1: Start by clicking the jobs tab in the navigation bar on myEDmatch.


ExpertTip: If you’re already on the dashboard, you can scroll down to the “Job Posting” section and click the button that says “+ Add a Job”

Back
Go to step 2

Step 2: Next, scroll down the page and click the "+ Add a Job" button.

Back
Go to step 3

Step 3: A window will appear for you to enter the information for the job posting you're creating. If you're part of a network of schools you'll want to make sure you're selecting the correct campus in the "school name" drop down. All fields in the job window are required except for "Minimum Salary."

If you're largely duplicating information from another job posting, you can select existing jobs from the drop-down menu and this posting will pre-populate with all of the information from your other existing jobs. You’ll just need to supply the new Start Date.

ExpertTip: As a convenience feature, we give you the option of setting up an auto-screener for applicants so you don’t receive notifications around low-fit candidates. You can set up an auto-reject threshold by selecting the lowest Match Score for which you want to consider teachers applying for your jobs. Candidates with Match Scores lower than that threshold will receive an email 24 hours after applying that notifies them that they are not moving further along in the application process, and you as the school user will not receive any notification emails or messages from any teachers who express interest in your jobs but do not meet the minimum Match Score criteria, saving you time on profile screening.

Back
Go to step 4

Step 4: Click "Save" at the bottom of the window. Be sure to tell your teachers to indicate that they work at your school during the registration process. When your school’s teachers join myEDmatch, they will then appear in the dropdown list of staff members you can associate with your school so you can highlight them on your Public Profile.

Back
Go to step 5

Step 5: Begin sharing your jobs unique URL on social media and your school's careers page. Our Director of Technical Development, Lisa French (lisa.french@myedmatch.com) can assist you in linking your jobs directly through an “Apply Now” button and associated widget, so if you’d like assistance integrating your existing systems with myEDmatch as seamlessly as possible, please don’t hesitate to reach out to her. As an additional free convenience feature, we cross-post your jobs to 10 major jobs boards including Indeed and CareerBuilder entirely free of charge to you.

ExpertTip: When you click on the job posting, you can edit it by clicking “Edit” in the top right corner of the Job posting. If you fill a position, make sure to Close/Archive that position. This can be done either from the job description itself by clicking “Archive” in the upper right hand corner, or quickly from the Dashboard (by changing the status from “Active” to “Closed.” Archiving your jobs that have already been filled will prevent your jobs tab from becoming cluttered. Archiving a job will not delete it, it will just store it on a different tab in your “Jobs” page so you can focus your attention on the jobs you still actively need to fill.
